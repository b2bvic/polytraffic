# Traffic Portfolio Risk Calculator

## How Concentrated Is Your Traffic? Find Out in 3 Minutes.

---

### THE PROBLEM

You check Google Search Console before coffee. A 15% dip sends your heart rate spiking. Yesterday's graph was flat—you exhaled. Today it's down—now you're scanning Reddit for algorithm update rumors.

This isn't a traffic problem. It's a concentration problem.

Your site runs on one channel. Maybe 75% Google organic. Maybe 80%. You've built a business on rented land, and the landlord changes the rules every four months.

The September 2023 Helpful Content Update wiped out 60% of affiliate organic traffic in 48 hours. March 2024 Core Update hammered informational sites. November 2024 spam update caught legitimate publishers in crossfire.

Every 4-6 months, Google drops something. Every time, the same sites get crushed: the ones running 70%+ from a single channel.

You know you should diversify. You've tried. Pinterest flopped. Paid ads burned money. You went back to SEO because at least you know the rules.

Now you're stuck. Fragile. Checking Search Console like it's a slot machine.

---

### THE MECHANISM

Portfolio theory applies to traffic the same way it applies to stocks.

Harry Markowitz won a Nobel Prize proving that diversification reduces risk without proportionally reducing returns. The math works for any asset class—equities, bonds, real estate. Or traffic sources.

The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) measures concentration in a portfolio. Used by economists to assess market monopoly risk. Works identically for traffic allocation.

**HHI Calculation:**
Square each channel's percentage share, sum the results.

- 90% Google, 10% email = (90)² + (10)² = 8,100 + 100 = **8,200** (extreme concentration)
- 60% Google, 25% email, 10% Pinterest, 5% paid = (60)² + (25)² + (10)² + (5)² = 3,600 + 625 + 100 + 25 = **4,350** (moderate)
- 40% Google, 30% email, 20% social, 10% paid = (40)² + (30)² + (20)² + (10)² = 1,600 + 900 + 400 + 100 = **3,000** (diversified)

**Risk Thresholds:**
| HHI Score | Risk Level | Interpretation |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| 0-2,500 | Low | Well-diversified, algorithm-resilient |
| 2,500-5,000 | Moderate | Acceptable, monitor for concentration drift |
| 5,000-7,500 | High | Vulnerable to single-channel disruption |
| 7,500+ | Extreme | One algorithm update away from crisis |

Concentration alone doesn't tell the full story. Correlation matters.

Google Organic + Google Ads = same platform, same algorithm influence, **high correlation**. When one drops, both drop. That's not diversification—it's the same bet twice.

Google Organic + Email List = different platforms, zero algorithm overlap, **low correlation**. When organic tanks, email holds steady. That's a hedge.

---

### THE SOLUTION

This assessment measures three risk factors:

**1. Channel Concentration (HHI Score)**
How much of your traffic comes from your largest source? How distributed is the rest?

**2. Correlation Risk**
Are your channels on the same platform? Same algorithm? Same company ownership? High-correlation channels fail together.

**3. Owned vs. Rented Ratio**
Email is owned—you control it. Social is earned—you've built an audience. SEO is rented—Google controls the terms. Paid is temporary—it stops when you stop paying.

The assessment outputs:
- Your current HHI score and risk level
- Channel correlation map showing which sources move together
- Owned/rented ratio and what it means for resilience
- Target allocation based on your business model
- Prioritized action list for reducing concentration

## SCORING

### Calculate Your HHI Score

Using your actual traffic percentages (from analytics), calculate:

```
HHI = (Google Organic %)² + (Email %)² + (Paid %)² + (Social %)² + (Referral %)² + (Direct %)² + (Other %)²
```

**Example:**
- Google Organic: 72%
- Email: 8%
- Paid: 0%
- Social: 5%
- Referral: 3%
- Direct: 12%

HHI = (72)² + (8)² + (0)² + (5)² + (3)² + (12)² = 5,184 + 64 + 0 + 25 + 9 + 144 = **5,426** (High Risk)

### If Your Correlation Risk is High (6+)

**Immediate priorities:**

1. **Separate Google exposure**
   - If running SEO + Google Ads: reduce Ads dependency, not SEO
   - Build alternative paid channel (Meta, Reddit, programmatic)

2. **Add platform-independent channel**
   - Email list (zero Google dependency)
   - Newsletter partnerships (cross-promotion with non-competitors)
   - Direct referral traffic (guest posts, podcast appearances)

3. **Monitor correlation during next update**
   - Track all channels daily for 7 days post-update
   - Document which channels moved together
   - Adjust allocation based on observed correlation
