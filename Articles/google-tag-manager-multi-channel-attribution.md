# Newsletter Platforms Traffic Comparison: beehiiv vs Substack vs Ghost vs ConvertKit

> **Quick Summary**
> - **What this covers:** Compare newsletter platform features for driving traffic back to content sites. Analyze beehiiv, Substack, Ghost, and ConvertKit for referral traffic generation and subscriber monetization.
> - **Who it's for:** traffic strategists and growth operators
> - **Key takeaway:** Read the first section for the core framework, then use the specific tactics that match your situation.


**Newsletter platforms** generate traffic to content sites when used strategically. But platforms differ dramatically in how they handle external links, traffic attribution, and referral patterns.

Some platforms encourage sending traffic to your site. Others want subscribers consuming content within the platform. This fundamental difference shapes which platform works best for content publishers.

This analysis compares beehiiv, Substack, Ghost, and ConvertKit specifically for traffic generation and referral capabilities.

## Platform Philosophy on External Traffic

**beehiiv** (traffic-friendly):
- Encourages linking to external content
- No penalties for driving subscribers off-platform
- Built for content marketers who own websites
- Easy UTM parameter implementation
- Designed as distribution channel, not content destination

**Substack** (platform-first):
- Encourages publishing full content within Substack
- External links allowed but not emphasized
- Subscriber discovery happens within Substack network
- Platform wants to be content destination
- Harder to drive systematic traffic to external sites

**Ghost** (publisher-controlled):
- Complete flexibility on external linking
- No platform agenda on where traffic should go
- Self-hosted option provides total control
- Built for independent publishers
- Neutral on external traffic vs platform content

**ConvertKit** (traffic-neutral):
- Email service provider, not publishing platform
- Agnostic about where subscribers go
- Tools support external linking easily
- No network effects encouraging platform publishing
- Designed as infrastructure, not destination

**Strategic implication:** Publishers primarily seeking traffic generation should prioritize beehiiv, Ghost, or ConvertKit. Publishers building platform-native audiences may prefer Substack.

## Traffic Generation Capabilities

### beehiiv Traffic Features

**Link placement:**
- Unlimited links within newsletters
- Click tracking on all links
- UTM parameters auto-append to external links
- Link analytics showing clicks per link

**Referral traffic volume:**
- Typical CTR: 3-8% of opens
- 10K subscribers, 45% open rate = 4,500 opens
- 5% CTR = 225 site visits per send
- Weekly newsletter = ~900 monthly referral visits

**Attribution clarity:**
- Source: beehiiv
- Medium: email
- Campaign: customizable per send
- Clean attribution in Google Analytics

**Traffic quality:**
- High engagement (newsletter readers are qualified)
- Lower bounce rates than cold traffic
- Higher pages per session than social traffic

### Substack Traffic Features

**Link placement:**
- Links allowed but full content publishing encouraged
- No automatic UTM parameter appending
- Manual link tracking setup required
- Analytics show clicks but less detailed than beehiiv

**Referral traffic volume:**
- Typical CTR: 2-5% of opens (lower than beehiiv)
- Subscribers often consume content within Substack
- 10K subscribers = 400-600 monthly referral visits
- Lower traffic generation than email-first platforms

**Attribution challenges:**
- Substack domain in referrer, not subscriber's email client
- All traffic appears as "substack.com" referral
- Difficult to segment by individual newsletter performance
- Manual UTM parameters essential for proper tracking

**Traffic quality:**
- Very high engagement (subscribers pay or choose to follow)
- Lower volume but higher intent
- Better for premium audience, worse for scale traffic

### Ghost Traffic Features

**Link placement:**
- Complete control (you own the infrastructure)
- Unlimited links, no restrictions
- Custom UTM parameter implementation
- Full analytics integration options

**Referral traffic volume:**
- Typical CTR: 4-9% of opens
- Higher than Substack, competitive with beehiiv
- 10K subscribers = 800-1,000+ monthly referral visits
- Email design and optimization determine CTR

**Attribution control:**
- Custom domain for email sending (your brand)
- UTM parameters fully customizable
- Server-side tracking possible
- Best attribution control of any platform

**Traffic quality:**
- Depends entirely on list quality and content
- Platform doesn't influence quality positively or negatively
- Publishers control every variable affecting traffic

### ConvertKit Traffic Features

**Link placement:**
- Unlimited links and complete flexibility
- Visual automation builder connects links to sequences
- Link triggers enable behavioral automation
- Click analytics per link and per subscriber

**Referral traffic volume:**
- Typical CTR: 4-8% of opens
- Strong traffic generation capability
- 10K subscribers = 800-1,200 monthly referral visits
- Automation enables consistent traffic without manual sends

**Attribution options:**
- Manual UTM parameter implementation required
- Integration with GA4 and analytics platforms
- Custom domain email sending available
- Good attribution with proper setup

**Traffic quality:**
- Behavioral segmentation enables high-intent traffic
- Subscribers clicking specific links enter relevant sequences
- Better traffic quality through segmentation than platforms without automation

## Subscriber Growth and Network Effects

**Substack network effects:**
- Substack recommends newsletters to subscribers
- Writers cross-promote within platform
- Discovery tab surfaces new newsletters
- Typical growth: 2-5% monthly from internal discovery
- Platform provides distribution assistance

**beehiiv growth tools:**
- Referral program builder (built-in virality)
- Paid ad network for subscriber acquisition
- Recommendation features between newsletters
- Typical growth: 1-3% monthly from platform features
- Strong growth infrastructure

**Ghost growth:**
- No built-in network effects or discovery
- Growth comes entirely from your marketing
- Typical growth: 0% from platform (100% from publisher effort)
- Complete independence but no platform assistance

**ConvertKit growth:**
- Minimal network effects
- Recommendation widget available
- Primarily grow through external marketing
- Typical growth: 0-1% monthly from platform features
- Focus is on conversion tools, not discovery

**Implication:** Publishers needing growth assistance should consider beehiiv or Substack. Publishers with strong existing traffic should consider Ghost or ConvertKit.

## Monetization Impact on Traffic Strategy

**Substack paid subscriptions:**
- Writers paywall content on Substack
- Less incentive to drive traffic to external sites
- Monetization happens within platform
- Traffic generation becomes secondary objective

**beehiiv ad network:**
- Monetize through beehiiv's ad network
- Revenue comes from newsletter itself, not external traffic
- Can pursue both newsletter ads and site traffic
- Flexible monetization supporting multiple strategies

**Ghost memberships:**
- Paywall content on your Ghost site
- Strong incentive to drive traffic to membership pages
- Monetization requires external traffic
- Traffic generation aligns with monetization

**ConvertKit commerce:**
- Sell products via ConvertKit commerce features
- Revenue comes from product sales, not newsletter
- Traffic drives to product pages
- Traffic generation critical for monetization

**Strategic fit:**
- Substack: Best for platform-native paid newsletters
- beehiiv: Best for hybrid newsletter + site monetization
- Ghost: Best for driving membership/site traffic
- ConvertKit: Best for product-focused email funnels

## FAQ

**Which newsletter platform drives most traffic to external websites?**

Ghost and beehiiv, roughly tied. Ghost provides most control and customization. beehiiv offers best balance of traffic features and ease-of-use. ConvertKit competitive through automation. Substack lowest for external traffic due to platform-first philosophy.

**Can publishers use Substack while still driving traffic to their site?**

Yes, but Substack's design discourages this. No automatic UTM tracking, limited analytics, and platform network effects favor publishing content within Substack. Publishers serious about site traffic should consider alternatives.

**Should publishers choose platforms based on traffic generation or subscriber growth?**

Depends on business model. Publishers monetizing via site traffic (ads, affiliates) need platforms optimized for traffic generation. Publishers monetizing via paid subscriptions can choose platforms optimized for subscriber growth even if external traffic suffers.

**Can publishers migrate between platforms without losing subscribers?**

Yes. All platforms allow list exports. Technical migration is straightforward. Psychological migration harder—subscribers may resist changing platforms, particularly paid Substack subscribers. Expect 10-30% churn during platform migrations.

**Do newsletter platforms affect email deliverability and open rates?**

Yes significantly. beehiiv and ConvertKit typically achieve 40-50% open rates with good content. Substack slightly lower at 35-45%. Ghost highly variable depending on email service provider configuration. Deliverability infrastructure matters enormously for traffic generation.

## When This Analysis Doesn't Apply

Skip this framework if:

- **You're in the first 3 months of a new site.** Traffic diversification assumes you have at least one working channel. Establish your first reliable traffic source before optimizing the portfolio.
- **Your traffic is already diversified below 40% from any single source.** You've solved the concentration problem. Focus on channel efficiency and conversion optimization instead.
- **You're running a time-limited campaign.** Short-term projects (product launches, events) benefit from channel concentration, not diversification. Spread resources after the sprint.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How quickly can I implement this traffic strategy?

Most frameworks in this article can be partially deployed within a week. Full implementation with measurement infrastructure typically takes 2-4 weeks. Start with the diagnostic steps before committing to major channel shifts.

### Does this work for sites with less than 10K monthly visitors?

Yes. The principles apply at any traffic level. Smaller sites benefit more from channel diversification because single-source dependency is riskier with a smaller base. The measurement approach scales down — start with simpler attribution before building complex models.

### What tools do I need to execute this?

Google Search Console and Google Analytics cover the baseline. For deeper analysis: Ahrefs or Semrush for competitive data, a spreadsheet for channel attribution tracking. No enterprise tools required — the strategy is more important than the tooling.
